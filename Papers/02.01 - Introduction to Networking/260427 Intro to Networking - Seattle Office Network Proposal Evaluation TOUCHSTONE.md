### Overview

GreenLeaf Organic Foods is expanding into Seattle and needs a reliable, scalable network to support sales, operations, and connectivity to the Portland headquarters. In this Touchstone, you will evaluate three vendor proposals and recommend the best solution based on technical fit, cost, reliability, and business needs.

**What You Will Learn:** Touchstone Tasks 1–4 prepared you to interpret diagrams, analyze switching and wireless designs, evaluate network performance, and assess security controls. In this final Touchstone, you will apply those same analytical skills to evaluate vendor proposals for the Seattle office. You will compare WAN connectivity, routing, switching, wireless, security, scalability, and total cost of ownership using the Seattle Site Information and Vendor Proposal documents.  
  
**Why It Matters:** IT professionals must evaluate vendor proposals and make recommendations supported by evidence. This skill is essential for RFP assessments, procurement decisions, and technical leadership roles.  
  
**What You Will Hand In:** A 4–6 page single spaced proposal evaluation document (doc or docx) that includes your analysis, scoring matrix, and final recommendation.  
  
**Keys to Success:**

- Apply the analytical methods practiced in Touchstones 1–4 to evaluate vendor designs.
- Demonstrate clear understanding of Seattle network requirements (connectivity, redundancy, wireless coverage, security, scalability).
- Accurately compare Vendor A, B, and C using the evaluation categories.
- Use evidence from the Seattle Site Information, Vendor Proposals, and the Evaluation Guide
- Provide a justified, balanced, and realistic recommendation.
- Meet length and formatting requirements.

### Instructions

**Step 1: Review Touchstone Tasks 1–4**  
Before you begin, review the analyses you completed earlier in the course:

- _Touchstone 1_ — Network topology, IP addressing, protocol behavior. These skills help you evaluate vendor diagrams, addressing plans, WAN designs, and routing approaches.
- _Touchstone 2_ — Switching roles, VLAN design, trunking, and redundancy. These skills help you judge vendor LAN architecture, segmentation, and scalability.
- _Touchstone 3_ — Wireless design, channel planning, baselines, and monitoring. These skills help you evaluate wireless coverage, AP counts, and performance expectations.
- _Touchstone 4_ — Security zones, firewall rules, VPN settings, authentication, and hardening. These skills help you assess security posture, encryption, remote access, and compliance.

You are not re-submitting these Touchstones, but you should use the same analytical reasoning as you evaluate each vendor.  
  
**Step 2: Analyze Each Vendor Proposal**  
Using the evaluation categories from the [Evaluation Guide](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/65576-Proposal_Evaluation_Guide.txt), review [Vendor A](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/63730-Vendor_A_Proposal.txt), [Vendor B](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/63731-Vendor_B_Proposal%20\(1\).txt), and [Vendor C](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/65561-Vendor_C_Proposal.txt). Apply the same techniques you used in Touchstones 1–4 when assessing [Seattle Site Information](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/63733-Seattle_Site_Information.txt) and [Seattle Network Requirements](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/63734-Seattle_Requirements.txt) to evaluate whether each vendor meets::

1. _Technical Requirements:_ LAN, WLAN, WAN design, addressing, security controls
2. _Scalability:_ VLAN structure, switching capacity, wireless density, WAN growth
3. _Reliability and Redundancy':' failover, HA options, dual uplinks, hardware selection_
4. _Total Cost of Ownership (5 Years):_ hardware, licensing, support costs, replacement cycle
5. _Vendor Capability:_ experience, implementation maturity, long-term support  
    

Support your evaluation with specific evidence from the Seattle Site Information and vendor proposal documents.  
  
**Step 3: Complete the Scoring Matrix**  
Using the weighted scoring system from the Evaluation Guide:

- Assign a score (1–5) for each vendor in every category.
- Multiply by the category weight.
- Calculate total weighted scores.

Include your completed scoring matrix in the final submission.  
  
**Step 4: Write Your Final Recommendation**  
Use the [writing framework template](https://app.sophia.org/download/attachment/63727-ITN%20Unit%205%20Touchstone%20Final%20Evaluation%20Student%20Template.docx) to structure your recommendation. Your submission must include:

1. Executive Summary
2. Analysis Summary
3. Strengths of Your Recommended Vendor
4. Limitations and Mitigations
5. Implementation Considerations
6. Alternatives Considered

Your reasoning should draw directly from:

- Seattle requirements
- Vendor proposal details
- Scoring matrix results
- Analytical frameworks practiced in Touchstones 1–4

Common Pitfalls to Avoid:

- CHEAPEST ISN'T ALWAYS BEST
    - Consider total value, not just price
    - Factor in reliability and support costs
    - Calculate cost of downtime
- MOST EXPENSIVE ISN'T ALWAYS BEST
    - Over-engineering wastes resources
    - Match solution to actual needs
    - Don't pay for features you won't use
- IGNORING HIDDEN COSTS
    - Licensing renewals
    - Support contracts
    - Training requirements
    - Growth costs
- OVERLOOKING OPERATIONAL IMPACT
    - Can current IT staff manage it?
    - Is training included?
    - How complex is daily operation?
- FOCUSING ONLY ON DAY 1
    - Consider 3-5 year horizon
    - Plan for growth
    - Think about technology lifecycle
